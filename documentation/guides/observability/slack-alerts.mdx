## Overview

Cekura can send real-time Slack notifications when metrics fail on live production calls ingested through observability. Each alert includes a link to the call in your dashboard so your team can review and act immediately.

## How It Works

When a production call is evaluated and a metric fails, Cekura sends a Slack alert to your connected channel. The notification includes a **Go to call** button that links directly to the call in the Cekura observability view.

<Note>
Slack alerts are metric-based — a notification fires for each metric failure, not for every call ingested. To receive a notification for every call regardless of result, see [Getting notified for every call](#getting-notified-for-every-call) below.
</Note>

## Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect your Slack workspace">
    Navigate to **Settings** → **Integrations** → **Slack** in your Cekura dashboard and connect your workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite the Cekura bot to your channel">
    In Slack, run `/invite @Cekura` in the channel where you want to receive alerts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable notifications in your project settings">
    In your Cekura project settings, enable Slack notifications. Alerts will now fire for any metric failure on a live production call.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Getting Notified for Every Call

By default, a Slack notification is only sent when a metric fails. If you want your channel to receive an alert for every ingested production call, create a dedicated metric whose evaluation criteria always returns a failure. Attach this metric to your observability agent — every call will trigger it, generating a corresponding Slack alert for each one.

## Submitting Feedback from Slack

You can provide metric feedback directly from a Slack alert without opening the dashboard. When an alert arrives, click the 👎 button next to **Go to call** to open a feedback modal and flag an incorrect metric evaluation. The feedback is added to Metric Optimizer for refining your metric.
