## What You'll Learn

How to use metric sampling to reduce evaluation costs at scale while maintaining statistical accuracy.

## The Problem

At high call volumes, evaluating every metric on every call gets expensive — especially for LLM-based metrics. Sampling lets you evaluate a percentage of calls while still getting reliable trend data.

## How Sampling Works

Cekura uses a deterministic accumulator that ensures the exact percentage of calls are evaluated over time. With 30% sampling, exactly 30 out of every 100 calls get evaluated.

When a metric is skipped:
- A placeholder record is created (for audit trail)
- The explanation shows `"Sampling skipped"`
- No score is computed for that call

## Set Up Sampling

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set Project Sampling Rate">
    Go to **Settings** in your project and find **Metric Sampling Rate (%)**.

    Enter a value:
    - `100` — all calls evaluated (no sampling)
    - `30` — 30% of calls evaluated
    - `0.1` — 0.1% evaluated (for very high volume)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable Per Metric">
    Go to **Metrics** and toggle the **Sampling** option on individual metrics.

    Not all metrics need sampling.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to Sample (and What Not To)

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Enable Sampling" icon="check" color="#22C55E">
    - Expensive LLM-based metrics
    - Non-critical trend metrics
    - High-volume agent evaluations
  </Card>
  <Card title="Keep at 100%" icon="xmark" color="#EF4444">
    - Critical workflow metrics (Boolean pass/fail)
    - Low-cost metrics (latency, duration)
    - Metrics where you need every call evaluated
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Practical Strategy

| Environment | Sampling Rate | Rationale |
|------------|--------------|-----------|
| Development | 100% | Full coverage during testing |
| Staging | 100% | Catch all issues before production |
| Production (low volume) | 100% | Cost is manageable |
| Production (high volume) | 10-30% | Sufficient for trend analysis |

<Tip>
Start at 100% during development and reduce as you scale to production. The exact threshold depends on your call volume and budget.
</Tip>
