## What are Agents on Cekura?

Agents represent your AI voice or chat agent within Cekura. They hold the configuration Cekura needs to connect to your agent, run evaluators against it, and collect observability data from production calls.

<Tip>
  An "Agent" in Cekura refers to **your** voice or chat AI agent that you are testing — not the evaluators (testing agents) that simulate callers.
</Tip>

## Types of Agents

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Voice Agents" icon="microphone">
    Talk and listen during real-time calls. Tested via phone (PSTN), SIP, LiveKit, or Pipecat.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Chat Agents" icon="comment">
    Handle text-based conversations. Tested via WebSocket or provider-specific chat integrations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="SMS & WhatsApp Agents" icon="message">
    Send and receive text messages via SMS or WhatsApp-based flows.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Getting Started

Setting up an agent on Cekura follows three steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create an Agent">
    Add your agent's name, language, and description.

    [Go to Create an Agent](/documentation/key-concepts/agents/create-agent)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure Your Agent">
    Select your voice/chat provider and connect your integration settings.

    [Go to Configuring Your Agent](/documentation/key-concepts/agents/configure-agent)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Additional Settings (Optional)">
    Set up transcript enrichment, knowledge base, mock tools, dynamic variables, and inbound/outbound mode.

    [Go to Additional Settings](/documentation/key-concepts/agents/additional-settings)
  </Step>
</Steps>
