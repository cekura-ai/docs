## What You'll Learn

How to use Metric Lab to identify metric inaccuracies, annotate corrections, and auto-optimize your metric definitions.

## The Problem

Your metrics will have false positives (metric says pass when it should fail) and false negatives (metric says fail when it should pass). Metric Lab helps you systematically fix these.

## The Optimization Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify Mismatches">
    After running metrics on calls, review results and spot where the metric verdict doesn't match reality.

    - **False Positive**: Metric says "Appointment Booked" but agent didn't confirm the time
    - **False Negative**: Metric says "Not Booked" but agent actually completed the booking
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add to Lab">
    Select the problematic calls and click **Add to Lab**. Assign them to the relevant metric's lab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run Initial Baseline">
    In the Lab, click **Run** to evaluate your current metric against the test set. This establishes your baseline score (e.g., 3/5 correct).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Annotate">
    This is the critical step. For each incorrect result:

    1. Find the row where **Actual Value** differs from reality
    2. **Update Expected Value** to what it should be
    3. **Add Notes** explaining *why* the metric was wrong

    **Example note:**
    ```
    The user said "Sounds good" which implicitly confirms the
    appointment. This should count as a successful booking.
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Auto Improve">
    Click **Auto Improve**. Cekura analyzes your annotations and rewrites the metric's definition to handle the edge cases you identified.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and Save">
    - View the diff (old vs. new metric definition)
    - Check the new score (e.g., improved from 3/5 to 5/5)
    - Click **Save** to push the optimized metric to production
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for Good Annotations

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Explain the Why" icon="lightbulb">
    Don't just flip the value. Explain *why* the metric was wrong so Auto Improve can generalize.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Annotate at Least 6 Calls" icon="chart-line">
    More annotated examples = better optimization. Aim for at least 6 diverse examples.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Include Edge Cases" icon="code-branch">
    Add calls that represent unusual but valid scenarios — implicit confirmations, partial completions, etc.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Re-run After Saving" icon="arrows-rotate">
    After saving an optimized metric, re-run to verify the score holds up on new data.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
With Slack integration, you can submit feedback directly from Slack alerts. Click the feedback button next to a failed metric to open a feedback modal, and it gets added to Metric Lab automatically.
</Tip>
