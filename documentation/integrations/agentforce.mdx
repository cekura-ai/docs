## Overview

Cekura can simulate calls against agents built on **Salesforce Agentforce**. Cekura authenticates to your Salesforce environment via OAuth 2.0 and invokes your Agentforce agent, then transcribes the conversation and runs your evaluators against it.

## Required credentials

The Agentforce integration requires four values, all available from the Connected App configured in your Salesforce org:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Client ID** | OAuth 2.0 client ID (Consumer Key) from your Salesforce Connected App |
| **Client Secret** | OAuth 2.0 client secret (Consumer Secret) from your Salesforce Connected App. Write-only — stored encrypted. |
| **Domain** | Your Salesforce org domain, e.g. `https://example.my.salesforce.com` |
| **Agent ID** | The Agentforce agent ID to invoke, starting with `0Xx…` |

## Configuring credentials in Cekura

1. Open your agent in the Cekura Dashboard and go to **Agent Settings**.
2. Select **Agentforce** in the provider grid.
3. Enter the **Client ID**, **Client Secret**, **Domain**, and **Agent ID**.
4. Click **Save Changes**.

## Updating credentials

If your Salesforce Connected App credentials are rotated or your org's integration user is updated, the existing client secret stored in Cekura becomes invalid. To reconfigure:

1. Retrieve the new **Consumer Secret** from your Salesforce Connected App (Setup → App Manager → your app → Manage Consumer Details).
2. In the Cekura Dashboard, open the relevant agent's settings.
3. Re-enter the new **Client Secret** in the Agentforce section.
4. Click **Save Changes**.
5. Trigger a fresh test run to confirm the connection is re-established.

<Note>
Re-running a previously failed run will not pick up the updated credentials — always trigger a new run after saving changes.
</Note>

If the error persists after updating the secret, try creating a new agent in Cekura with the updated credentials to ensure a clean credential state.

## Troubleshooting

### `invalid_client` / `invalid client credentials`

Cekura's connection to your Salesforce environment was rejected at the OAuth authentication step. Common causes:

- The **Client Secret** stored in Cekura is stale (the Connected App secret was rotated or regenerated).
- The **Client ID** or **Domain** is incorrect.
- The Connected App has been deauthorized or its session policy has expired.

**Fix:** Verify the credentials in your Salesforce Connected App and re-enter the correct values in Cekura agent settings (see [Updating credentials](#updating-credentials) above).

### `invalid_app_access` / user not admin approved

The OAuth user is not authorized to access the Connected App.

**Fix:** In Salesforce, navigate to Setup → Connected Apps → Manage Connected Apps → select your app → OAuth Policies. Ensure the **Permitted Users** policy is set to allow your integration user, and that the integration user's Profile or Permission Set is listed in the app's authorized profiles.
